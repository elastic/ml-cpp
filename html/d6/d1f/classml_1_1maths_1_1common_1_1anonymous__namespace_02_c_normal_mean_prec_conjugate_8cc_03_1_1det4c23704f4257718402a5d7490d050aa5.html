Detailed Description

The log marginal likelihood function of the samples is the log of the likelihood function for the data integrated over the prior distribution for the mean and scale. It can be shown that: log( L(x | m, p, a, b) ) = log( 1 / (2 * pi) ^ (n/2)

  • (p / (p + n)) ^ (1/2)
  • b ^ a
  • Gamma(a + n/2) / Gamma(a) / (b + 1/2 * (n * var(x) + p * n * (mean(x) - m)^2 / (p + n))) ^ (a + n/2) ).

Here, x = {x(i)} is the sample vector. n = |x| the number of elements in the sample vector. mean(x) is the sample mean. var(x) is the sample variance. m and p are the prior Gaussian mean and precision, respectively. a and b are the prior Gamma shape and rate, respectively.
